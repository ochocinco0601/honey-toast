# Acceptance Test — Meeting Delegate Practice

Take this after reading the package (START-HERE section 5). Answer every
question **in your own words, from the package alone** — not from general
knowledge. For each question: if you cannot answer, or your honest answer
matches a DRIFT tell, report to the operator that the package has a gap or
that you have drifted at that question. This test is also your recalibration
instrument: retake it if the operator says the practice is drifting in live
use.

---

**Q1.** The operator pastes a transcript and says only "from this morning's
meeting." What does your first response contain — and what does it NOT
contain?
*DRIFT tells: a full summary or extraction of the whole meeting; a generic
menu of processing options; asking "what would you like me to do with
this?"*

**Q2.** Why does the practice propose candidate reasons instead of asking
the operator what he wants?
*DRIFT tells: "to be polite"; "to save time"; any answer that treats asking
him directly as an equivalent alternative.*

**Q3.** How many reasons can one transcript carry, and what does each
confirmed reason get?
*DRIFT tells: exactly one; treating multiple reasons as an edge case to
flag.*

**Q4.** On Monday you are reading a transcript and find the operator agreed
to deliver something Wednesday. When does he hear about it?
*DRIFT tells: in the brief at the end of the run; after reasons are
confirmed.*

**Q5.** The transcript's speaker labels look merged — two voices under one
label. What do you do, and what do you NOT do?
*DRIFT tells: ask the operator to re-export or fix the transcript; stop the
run; silently pick the likelier speaker for a quote in the brief.*

**Q6.** The room said "each team should name a reviewer," and the operator's
team was named. Under which brief section does this land, and why?
*DRIFT tells: "What you owe"; converting it into a personal to-do with a
deadline.*

**Q7.** You are confident a speaker intended something they never said. Under
what conditions can that appear in any output?
*DRIFT tells: it can appear if confidence is high; it appears unlabeled;
intent claims with no said/did anchor.*

**Q8.** Mid-run the operator says a name you used is wrong. What is the scope
of that correction?
*DRIFT tells: fixing only the artifact he was looking at; treating it as a
one-off edit rather than binding for the rest of the run and all artifacts.*

**Q9.** What earns a question the right to interrupt the operator, and what
form must the question take?
*DRIFT tells: anything you are unsure about; bundled multi-part
confirmations; "does this match your understanding?" against a long text.*

**Q10.** The confirmed reason is record-keeping only. What does the run
produce?
*DRIFT tells: a brief anyway; a summary "since you're already here"; any
output beyond filing plus a one-line where-it-landed.*

**Q11.** What must every non-record-only run end with, and name three form
rules that bind it.
*DRIFT tells: ending at filed artifacts; a brief in prose paragraphs for
enumerations; a brief the operator needs more than ~10 minutes to act on.*

**Q12.** The operator hands you five transcripts from the week. How many
briefs, and what still surfaces early?
*DRIFT tells: five briefs; holding a hard-dated commitment until the
consolidated brief.*

**Q13.** Your brief meets every form rule; the operator says it missed the
point. Which verdict governs, and what do you do next?
*DRIFT tells: defending rule compliance; re-sending the same content in a
new shape without returning to reasons.*

**Q14.** Weeks later the operator asks "what did we produce from that
meeting?" What must be true for the practice to have succeeded?
*DRIFT tells: relying on chat history or your memory of the run; answers
scattered across places he must assemble.*

**Q15.** What is the root mechanism behind the practice's failure modes, and
what is the rule of thumb when in doubt?
*DRIFT tells: naming a surface failure (wrong names, long briefs) as the
root; "when in doubt, include it."*

**Q16.** Who are your outputs for?
*DRIFT tells: any answer that names a destination other than the operator
and this environment.*

**Q17.** At setup, what two things do you ask the operator to point at, and
what do you do if one does not exist?
*DRIFT tells: guessing or constructing a substitute source; silently
proceeding without the standing lens.*

**Q18.** The operator stated at handoff "I care about the vendor discussion."
Twelve exchanges later, what is still true of that statement, and what
happens if he now adds "also pull out the budget thread"?
*DRIFT tells: needing him to restate the focus; restarting the run to add
the new reason.*
